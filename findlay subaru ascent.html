01The brief

The 3-row gap and why it matters.

The kids are 4, 6, and 9. The grandparents are coming for spring break and want to drive to Zion together. Eight people, six suitcases, one cooler, plus a 110-degree weekend ahead. The Subaru Ascent is one of the few 3-row SUVs that handles the math without making someone sit on a lap.

The Subaru Ascent is a 3-row SUV with seating for 7 or 8. Symmetrical AWD is standard on every Subaru except BRZ. EyeSight Driver Assist Technology is standard on every Ascent trim. The Ascent runs a 2.4-liter turbocharged Boxer engine making 260 horsepower with a 5,000-pound towing capacity, the highest in the Subaru lineup. The Ascent is Subaru's only 3-row family SUV.

Subaru sells exactly one 3-row SUV in 2026: the Ascent. The Outback is a 5-passenger raised wagon. The Forester is a 5-passenger crossover. The Solterra is a 5-passenger electric SUV. If a Las Vegas family needs to seat 7 or 8 plus carry weekend gear to Cedar Breaks or Bryce Canyon, the Ascent is the Subaru answer.

That gap matters because Las Vegas household sizes skew larger than the national average. The valley has a meaningful share of multi-generational households. Blended families with kids in two or three age brackets are common. Henderson and Summerlin parents routinely carpool three friends to soccer practice. A two-row crossover doesn't solve the third-soccer-mom problem. A 3-row does.

03Captain's vs bench

Captain's chairs or 8-passenger bench.

The single most important decision on a 2026 Ascent is the second-row layout: captain's chairs (7 passengers) or bench (8 passengers). Premium and Limited offer both. Limited Bronze Edition through Onyx Edition Touring are 7-passenger only.

Captain's chairs (7 passengers)

Standard on Limited Bronze Edition through Onyx Edition Touring; optional on Premium and Limited. Each second-row seat is a separate captain's chair with its own armrest. A walk-through aisle between the two chairs gives third-row passengers a clean path into row 3. Pick captain's chairs if your typical row-2 occupants are car-seat-age children whose seats can sit on the captain's chair safely (most convertible and infant car seats fit).

Bench (8 passengers)

Available on Premium and Limited. The 60/40 split bench seats three across in row 2. Pick the bench if you genuinely need 8 seats often (carpool runs, sports teams, extended-family trips), or if you prefer the option to fit three convertible car seats across row 2.

Third-row legroom: 31.7 inches

That number is competitive against the Honda Pilot's 32.5 inches and substantially better than the Toyota Highlander's 27.7 inches. Third-row adult comfort on the Ascent is best described as fine for a 30-minute Vegas-to-Lake-Mead trip, tight for the 4 hours to Bryce Canyon. For weekly use by adult third-row passengers, the Pilot is marginally roomier; for occasional adult use and regular use by kids 8 to 12, the Ascent works.

All three rows are car-seat-compatible, but check the third row. Cars.com's most recent Ascent Car Seat Check (2025) praises the second row (easy LATCH access, rigid buckle stalks, accommodating for child seats) but flags the third row as cramped with awkward buckle access. Only the driver-side third-row seat has exposed lower LATCH anchors.

04The hardware

The 2.4L turbo, AWD, and EyeSight.

Every 2026 Ascent uses the 2.4-liter turbocharged Boxer four-cylinder, which makes 260 horsepower and 277 pound-feet of torque, paired with Lineartronic CVT and Symmetrical AWD as standard equipment.

Engine

The 2.4L turbo is the same engine family that powers the Outback Wilderness. It has been in Subaru production since 2019. The torque curve is tuned for moving a heavy 3-row family vehicle plus 5,000 pounds of trailer, not for spirited acceleration.

Transmission and AWD

Lineartronic continuously variable transmission with 8-speed manual mode, paired with Symmetrical All-Wheel Drive standard on every trim. The 2.4L turbo produces an EPA-rated 19 city / 26 highway / 22 combined MPG on Premium, and 19 / 25 / 21 on the Limited and higher trims (per fueleconomy.gov 2026).

Towing: 5,000 pounds, the highest in the Subaru lineup

A properly equipped Ascent (Class III hitch installed) tows 5,000 pounds. That covers a 24-foot pontoon boat plus tandem trailer, a small-to-mid travel trailer (Coachmen Catalina 184BHS or Forest River R-Pod 196), or a two-horse bumper-pull trailer. The integrated trailer brake controller wiring is standard from Premium up. The controller itself is a dealer-installed accessory.

Ground clearance: 8.7 inches

Same as the Outback (non-Wilderness) and Forester (non-Wilderness). Higher than the Toyota Highlander (8.0 inches) or the Honda Pilot (7.3 inches all-trim, 8.3 inches TrailSport). The Ascent's clearance plus AWD plus X-MODE handles the unpaved roads to Lake Mead boat ramps, Valley of Fire State Park, and the gravel approach to Cedar Breaks National Monument.

EyeSight Driver Assist Technology

Standard on every Ascent trim. Two color stereo cameras at the top of the windshield provide adaptive cruise control, pre-collision braking, lane departure and sway warning, lane centering assist, and lead-vehicle start alert. Touring trim adds DriverFocus, a cabin-facing camera that detects distraction or drowsiness and uses face recognition for stored driver profiles.

06Family logistics

19 cup holders, 8 USB ports, a panoramic moonroof.

Subaru engineered the Ascent for family logistics rather than performance bragging rights. The detail count tells the story.

19 cup holders

Yes, nineteen. Two in front. Two in the second-row armrest. Four in the second-row doors and seatbacks. Four in the third-row sidewalls and armrests. Plus seven additional bottle holders distributed across all three rows. That's roughly 2.4 cup holders per passenger when fully loaded.

Up to 8 USB ports

One USB-A and one USB-C in the front row. One USB-A and one USB-C in the second row. Two USB-A in the third row. Every passenger seat has charging access. Touring and Onyx Edition Touring add a 120V household-style power outlet in the cargo area.

Panoramic moonroof

Standard on Limited, Limited Bronze Edition through Onyx Edition Touring (no package needed for Limited). The two-pane glass roof spans the second and third rows, which materially reduces the third-row "I'm in a cave" feeling on long trips. Premium does not offer a moonroof.

Hands-free power liftgate

All 2026 Ascents have a standard power rear gate; the hands-free, kick-to-open variant is standard from Limited up.

Captain's-chair walk-through

When second-row captain's chairs are equipped, the aisle between them gives third-row passengers a path that doesn't require folding row 2 forward.

X-MODE for the dirt-road family weekend

Standard on every Ascent trim. X-MODE retunes throttle, transmission, and brake-hold logic for low-traction surfaces (sand, mud, packed snow) at speeds under 25 MPH. It's a button on the center console; press it before turning onto the unpaved section.

Ascent vs Pilot

Pilot wins on 3rd-row legroom (32.5 vs 31.7 in) and cargo behind row 3. Ascent wins on standard AWD across every trim (Pilot charges $2,100 for AWD on Sport and EX-L), on non-TrailSport ground clearance (8.7 vs 7.3 in), and on Subaru's service-department continuity. Both tow 5,000 lb on AWD trims. Honda refreshed the Pilot for 2026 with a 12.3-inch screen and wireless CarPlay/Android Auto now standard; base price up about $2,000.

Ascent vs Highlander

Toyota ships AWD as standard on every 2026 Highlander trim, with no upcharge. Highlander is shorter (easier to park) and offers a hybrid rated 35 MPG combined AWD that the Ascent does not match. Ascent wins on 3rd-row legroom (31.7 vs 27.7 in, the 4-inch difference between "kids only" and "adults briefly") and on ground clearance (8.7 vs 8.0 in). For a Las Vegas household whose third row will routinely seat adults, the Ascent is the cleaner pick.

Ascent vs Telluride

Kia skipped MY2026 entirely. The Telluride buyer in 2026 is choosing between the 2025 (last 1st-gen, 3.8L V6 / 291 hp) and the redesigned 2027 (turbo-4 274 hp gas or turbo-hybrid 329 hp; tow 5,000 lb gas / 4,500 lb hybrid). Ascent wins on standard AWD, on second-row captain's-chair availability, and on Subaru's factory-trained service network. The 2027 Telluride wins on the new hybrid powertrain and on premium-feel interior at higher trims.

10The questions families ask

Frequently asked questions about the Subaru Ascent.

What is the 2026 Subaru Ascent MSRP?

Premium $40,795, Limited $47,885, Limited Bronze Edition $48,995, Touring $51,165, and Onyx Edition Touring $51,995 (all MSRPs exclude destination). Subaru restructured the lineup for 2026: the Base trim is gone (Premium is the entry trim), and the standalone Onyx Edition has been replaced by Onyx Edition Touring.

Findlay charges a $499 doc fee plus Nevada sales tax and DMV registration; average dealer add-ons run roughly $149. For current dealer pricing on a specific Ascent in stock, see live Ascent inventory or call 702-495-2197.

What's the real Las Vegas summer fuel economy on a 2026 Ascent?

EPA-rated 19/26/22 city/hwy/combined on Premium and 19/25/21 on Limited, Limited Bronze Edition through Onyx Edition Touring (per fueleconomy.gov 2026).

Real-world Las Vegas summer numbers (June through September, 105 to 115 degree highs, AC running constantly with three rows of passengers) typically come in 1 to 2 MPG below EPA. For a 15,000-mile-per-year family using the Ascent as a primary vehicle, that works out to roughly 680 to 715 gallons of fuel per year.

Can I fit three car seats across the second row of an Ascent?

Yes, with narrow convertible car seats and the bench-seat configuration (8-passenger Premium or Limited). The Cars.com 2025 Ascent Car Seat Check praises the second row for easy LATCH access and rigid buckle stalks. Three across is feasible with narrow convertibles but tight.

The captain's-chair configuration (7-passenger Limited Bronze Edition, Touring, or Onyx Edition Touring) only fits two car seats in row 2 because the captain's chairs are individual seats. Row 3 also accepts car seats, but Cars.com flags the third row as cramped with awkward buckle access; only the driver-side third-row seat has exposed lower LATCH anchors.

Is third-row seating comfortable for adults in the Ascent?

The Ascent's third row offers 31.7 inches of legroom, which is comfortable for shorter trips (under an hour) for adults under 5'10" and comfortable for kids and teens on any-length trip. For comparison, the Honda Pilot offers 32.5 inches and the Toyota Highlander offers 27.7 inches.

The Ascent's panoramic moonroof option (standard on Limited and up) materially reduces the third-row claustrophobic feeling on long trips. For a household whose third-row passengers are routinely adults on 4-hour Cedar Breaks or Bryce Canyon trips, the Pilot is marginally roomier; for a household whose third row is mostly kids 8 to 12 with occasional adult use, the Ascent works.

Can a Subaru Ascent tow a small travel trailer for a Bryce Canyon road trip?

Yes. A properly equipped 2026 Ascent (Class III hitch installed) tows up to 5,000 pounds, which covers a 24-foot pontoon boat plus tandem trailer, a small travel trailer such as a Coachmen Catalina 184BHS or Forest River R-Pod 196 (both around 3,500 to 4,200 pounds dry), or a two-horse bumper-pull trailer.

The integrated trailer brake controller wiring is standard from Premium up; the brake controller itself is a Findlay-installed accessory. For travel trailers over 5,000 pounds, the Ascent is not the right tow vehicle and a body-on-frame truck or full-size SUV is the better choice.

Is the 2.4L turbo Boxer engine in the Ascent reliable for the long haul?

The 2.4-liter turbocharged Boxer engine in the Ascent has been in Subaru production since 2019. Early model-year (2019-2022) turbo platforms had documented turbo oil-control valve issues, which Subaru addressed via Technical Service Bulletins and revised parts.

Consumer Reports' published reliability data trends as follows: the 2024 Ascent rated below average; the 2025 Ascent rated about average with body and infotainment issues remaining; the 2026 model is too new for a fully populated CR reliability score. The Ascent's 5-year / 60,000-mile powertrain warranty covers the engine and transmission. Subaru Certified Pre-Owned vehicles add a 7-year / 100,000-mile powertrain extension.
